Uploading to the Steam Workshop

Please note that uploading to the Steam Workshop is available to Steam customers only. Redeem the Steam activation code you've received with your purchase to use the Steam Workshop.

You've made a great mod and now you're ready to share it with the world. Now what?

  1. Test your mod. Make sure everything works as expected. Mods that crash the game might be marked as "incompatible" and removed from the Workshop. Use the game's debugging tools, if necessary.
  2. Set up config.json. config.json determines the mod title, author, and description that are shown in-game; it is located in your mod's root directory. Change these to your liking, and increment the version number if you're updating your mod.
  3. Choose a preview image. The preview image is what shows up when browsing your mod on the Steam Workshop and is required when uploading. Good preview images are usually square, and up to 1000x1000px.
  4. Upload the content in-game. Open After the Collapse, and click Mods. Once you click "Upload Mod", you'll have the opportunity to select a local mod and click "Upload Selected Mod". Fill out all of the fields on the resulting page (make sure to select the mod you're updating if you're uploading a new version of an existing mod!), select your preview image, and agree to the legal terms to reveal the "Upload publicly" or "Upload friends-only" buttons.

Happy sharing! We can't wait to see what you've built.
